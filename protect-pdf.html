Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the PDF still open without asking for a password? +
The password protection added by PDF Maker uses standard AES-256 PDF encryption. However, some PDF readers (like browser-based viewers) may not enforce it fully. To properly test your password, open the file in Adobe Acrobat Reader or another dedicated PDF app — it will correctly prompt for a password.
How strong is the encryption? +
PDF Maker uses AES-256 encryption — the same standard used by banks and governments. It is extremely secure when combined with a strong password (12+ characters, mixed case, numbers and symbols).
Can I unlock a PDF without the password? +
No. This tool only removes password protection when you provide the correct password. It cannot crack or bypass unknown passwords.
Is my PDF uploaded to a server? +
No. Everything happens entirely in your browser. Your file and password are never sent anywhere.
What do the permission settings do? +
Even after entering the correct password, you can restrict what users can do — like preventing printing or copying text. These are standard PDF permissions supported by Adobe Reader and most dedicated PDF apps.
