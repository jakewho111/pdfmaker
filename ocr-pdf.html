Frequently Asked Questions

What is OCR? +
OCR stands for Optical Character Recognition. It reads the text inside an image or scanned document and converts it into actual digital text that can be searched, copied, and edited.
What types of files can I use? +
You can upload JPG, PNG, and WebP image files. For best results use clear, high-resolution images with good lighting and contrast.
Are my files uploaded to a server? +
No. OCR runs entirely in your browser using Tesseract.js — a JavaScript port of the Tesseract OCR engine. Your files never leave your device.
Why is the first scan slow? +
The first scan downloads the Tesseract OCR engine (~10MB) and language data files to your browser. After the first scan everything is cached and subsequent scans are much faster.
How accurate is the OCR? +
Accuracy depends on image quality. Clear, high-resolution images with black text on white background can achieve 95%+ accuracy. Blurry, rotated, or low-contrast images will have lower accuracy.
